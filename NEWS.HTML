World Pokémon Championship 2025 Announced

Jason Mitchell

Senior Gaming Correspondent

8 min read

The Pokémon Company International has officially announced the dates and location for the highly anticipated World Pokémon Championship 2025. The premier competitive Pokémon event will take place from August 15-17, 2025, at the Tokyo Metropolitan Gymnasium in Japan, bringing together the world's top Pokémon trainers for an unforgettable weekend of strategic battles and celebration.

This marks the first time since 2019 that the World Championship will return to Japan, the birthplace of the Pokémon franchise. Organizers expect record attendance with more than 5,000 competitors from over 50 countries participating across multiple game categories.

Expanded Competition Categories

The 2025 Championship will feature competitions in the Pokémon Trading Card Game, Pokémon Scarlet & Violet video games, Pokémon GO, and Pokémon UNITE. New this year is the addition of the Pokémon Masters EX mobile game competition, expanding the championship to five official tournament categories.

Tsunekazu Ishihara, President and CEO of The Pokémon Company, expressed his excitement about the upcoming event: "We are thrilled to bring the World Championship back to Japan, where Pokémon was born. The 2025 Championship will be our biggest and most inclusive event yet, showcasing the incredible skill and dedication of Pokémon players from around the globe."

"The World Championship represents the pinnacle of competitive Pokémon play. These trainers have dedicated countless hours to mastering their strategies, and we're excited to see who will emerge victorious."

Qualification Process

Players will need to earn Championship Points through regional tournaments and qualifying events throughout the 2024-2025 season to secure their invitation to the World Championship. The qualification period begins on July 1, 2024, and runs through June 30, 2025.

For the first time, the Championship will implement a new tiered qualification system that provides more opportunities for players from underrepresented regions to participate, ensuring a truly global competition.

Spectator Experience

While the competition remains the centerpiece of the event, the 2025 Championship is expanding its offerings for spectators and casual fans. The Pokémon Experience Zone will feature demo stations for upcoming games, photo opportunities with life-sized Pokémon statues, exclusive merchandise, and meet-and-greets with Pokémon celebrities and content creators.

Spectator tickets will go on sale on January 15, 2025, with both in-person and virtual attendance options available. The entire event will be livestreamed on the official Pokémon Twitch and YouTube channels with commentary in multiple languages.

Prize Pool and Sponsorships

The 2025 Championship boasts the largest prize pool in the event's history, with over $2 million in cash and prizes across all competition categories. The winners in each category will receive the prestigious Pokémon World Champion title, an exclusive trophy, and automatic qualification for the 2026 World Championship.

Several major sponsors have already signed on for the event, including Nintendo, The Pokémon Company International, and several new corporate partners that will be announced in the coming months.

Community Impact

Beyond the competition, the World Championship aims to make a positive impact on the local community. The Pokémon Company will partner with local organizations in Tokyo to host educational workshops on game design, programming, and digital art for students interested in pursuing careers in the gaming industry.

Additionally, a portion of the proceeds from merchandise sales will be donated to children's hospitals in Japan through the Pokémon With You Foundation, continuing the company's tradition of charitable giving.

As anticipation builds for this premier Pokémon event, players around the world are already intensifying their training regimens and refining their strategies. Whether you're a competitive player aiming for glory or a fan looking to experience the excitement firsthand, the 2025 World Pokémon Championship promises to be an unforgettable celebration of competitive Pokémon play and the global community that surrounds it.
